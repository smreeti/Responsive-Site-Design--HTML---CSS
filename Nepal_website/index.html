Nepal, a landlocked country in South Asia, is a multi-ethnic, multi-lingual, multi-cultural, and multi-religious state. It includes diverse geographical structures, fertile plains, forested hills, and eight out of the world's ten tallest mountains, including Mount Everest, with 8,848.86 meters being the highest on Earth. It is classified into three physical physiographic regions known as Himal, Pahad, and Terai.

Landform Regions

Himalayan Region

This region covers the northern part of Nepal and includes the highest elevations above 8000 peaks, such as Mount Everest, Lhotse, Makalu, Dhaulagiri, Annapurna, and Manasalu. The most significant feature of the Himalayas is their heights, topography, complex geographical structure, and combination of diverse climate, flora, and fauna.

Hilly Region

This region occupies the southern part of the Mountain region (called Pahad in Nepali) with around 1,000 and 4,000 meters altitude. It consists of the Mahabharat range and the Churia Hills. One of the significant features includes Kathmandu valley, the capital city of Nepal, which is the country's most fertilized and developed area.

Terai Region

This region is often known as the granary of Nepal because of its ability to cultivate and generate food products such as paddy, wheat, pulses, moong, sugarcane, jute, tobacco, and maize. Significantly, it consists of plenty of fertile lands, alluvial soil, and water suitable for farming and irrigation.
